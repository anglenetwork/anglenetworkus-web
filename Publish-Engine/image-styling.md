# Cover image styling catalog

Internal production catalog for Angle cover-image transforms.

**Default:** do **not** style cover images.

**Apply a style only when** the user explicitly requests visual styling, names a style from this catalog, or pastes a style prompt for the article.

After transform:

* Keep `creditAuthor`, `creditSource`, and `licenseOrRights` from the **original source photo page**.
* Write `caption` / `alt` about the photograph’s subject only.
* **Never** disclose the style name, effects, collage treatment, or AI processing in public fields.

Workflow notes: see [`instructions-to-publish.md`](instructions-to-publish.md) → Images → Cover image visual styling.

---

## Torn-paper / zine collages

High-energy, asymmetrical editorial collages. Strong black negative space, grain, and ripped-paper masks.

### 1. Red + cream + checkerboard blocks

**Prompt:**

```txt
Transform the uploaded image into a raw editorial torn-paper collage for a contemporary news publication. Preserve the original subject, recognizable identity, important objects, and essential factual meaning. Convert the main photograph into high-contrast black and white with grainy halftone and photocopy texture. Cut the photo into an irregular torn-paper fragment with a strong diagonal ripped edge. Layer it over offset paper panels in black, off-white, warm cream, muted beige, and vivid editorial red. Add a large red rectangular block behind the main image and a checkerboard strip near the bottom using cream and red squares. Include a secondary conceptual illustration related to the topic, rendered with rough black ink lines and halftone texture. Add a few small hand-drawn arrows, scribbles, and abstract symbols. Use rough paper edges, subtle print noise, and large areas of black negative space. Keep the result asymmetrical, energetic, intelligent, and magazine-like. No text, logos, or new factual elements.
```

### 2. Blue + gray + circles and arcs

**Prompt:**

```txt
Edit the uploaded image into a neo-editorial zine collage style. Preserve the subject’s identity, pose, important objects, and essential factual content. Render the main photo in high-contrast black and white with visible newsprint grain and halftone texture. Place it inside an irregular torn-paper cutout crossing the composition diagonally. Use layered panels in black, cool gray, off-white, powder blue, and deep cobalt blue. Add a bold blue circular shape and a few partial arcs or semicircles behind and around the collage layers. Replace the checkerboard motif with a modular band of circular dots or rounded tiles near the lower edge. Add one conceptual graphic in the upper-left area, using simple line drawing and halftone shading. Include a restrained number of loose ink loops and abstract marks. Keep strong black negative space, rough paper edges, and a premium editorial mood. Do not add text, branding, or extra people.
```

### 3. Orange + sand + vertical bars

**Prompt:**

```txt
Transform the uploaded image into a bold torn-paper editorial collage with a print-inspired zine aesthetic. Preserve the original subject and factual meaning. Convert the main image to grainy high-contrast black and white with subtle photocopied halftone texture. Mask the photograph inside a rough torn-paper fragment with an angled ripped edge. Build the composition with layered paper panels in black, off-white, pale sand, warm taupe, and burnt orange. Add a large burnt-orange block behind the image and use vertical bar shapes or elongated rectangular columns as the main supporting motif. Place a strip of alternating rectangular blocks near the bottom. Add a secondary conceptual illustration in the top portion of the image with rough line work and print texture. Include a few small hand-drawn symbols and subtle misregistration. Keep the layout asymmetrical, layered, tactile, and editorial, with generous black negative space. No text, logos, or invented factual details.
```

### 4. Purple + lilac + diamonds and diagonal panels

**Prompt:**

Turn the main photograph into high-contrast black and white with visible print grain and photocopy noise. Place it in a large ripped-paper shape cutting diagonally across the composition. Use a palette of black, soft gray, off-white, pale lilac, muted violet, and deep plum. In the upper-left section, place a conceptual illustration or symbolic graphic related to the story, rendered with rough ink lines and halftone texture. ## Minimalist editorial portrait collages

Cleaner, premium portrait treatments. Best when the cover centers on a recognizable person or strong single subject.

### 1. Red + cream + rectangular blocks

Bold and branded; strong fit for a news homepage.

**Prompt:**

```txt
Edit the uploaded image in a minimalist editorial collage style. Preserve the subject’s identity, facial features, pose, and essential composition. Apply a high-contrast monochrome portrait treatment with a subtle deep red duotone tint over the subject. Place the subject against a solid black rectangular background to create strong focus and separation. Surround the portrait with a clean abstract collage made of layered rectangular paper-like blocks in cream, ivory, soft beige, and vivid editorial red. Keep the layout modern, restrained, and premium, with subtle paper grain and print texture throughout. The design should feel like a serious contemporary magazine or newspaper cover, with minimal clutter and strong visual hierarchy. Avoid text, logos, extra objects, or overly decorative elements.
```

### 2. Teal + sand + circles and arcs

More modern and cultural/editorial.

**Prompt:**

```txt
Transform the provided image into a premium editorial portrait collage. Preserve the subject’s identity, expression, and pose. Render the portrait in a high-contrast black-and-white treatment with a subtle cool teal duotone tint. Place the portrait inside a deep black rectangular field. Around the main portrait area, create an abstract composition using soft circles, partial circles, arcs, and rounded geometric forms in sand, ivory, pale stone, muted teal, and deep teal. The shapes should feel like layered paper cutouts with slight overlap and subtle transparency. Add a light paper grain / print texture to give the image a tactile editorial feel. Keep the overall composition minimal, elegant, and magazine-like, avoiding clutter, surrealism, or commercial-ad aesthetics.
```

### 3. Orange + taupe + vertical bars

Analytical / feature-story feel.

**Prompt:**

```txt
Edit the image into a minimalist editorial collage style with a refined print-inspired aesthetic. Preserve the original subject and keep the face, pose, and features recognizable. Apply a high-contrast monochrome portrait treatment with a subtle warm burnt-orange duotone tint. Set the portrait against a solid black rectangular background. Build the surrounding composition using clean vertical bars and elongated rectangular shapes in taupe, warm beige, cream, muted clay, and burnt orange. The background should resemble layered printed-paper panels arranged in a structured, modern way. Include subtle grain and paper texture, and maintain a serious, elegant editorial tone. The result should look like a modern longform feature cover with strong negative space and minimal decoration.
```

### 4. Purple + lilac + diamonds / angled shapes

Artsy while still editorial.

**Prompt:**

```txt
Transform the uploaded image into a sophisticated editorial portrait collage. Preserve the subject’s identity, face, expression, and pose. Convert the portrait into a high-contrast monochrome image with a subtle violet duotone tint. Place the subject within a deep black rectangular portrait field. Around it, create a minimalist collage using diamond shapes, angled panels, and diagonal geometric pieces in off-white, pale gray-lilac, soft lavender, muted violet, and deep plum accents. The shapes should feel like layered paper elements rather than glossy digital graphics. Add a soft print grain / matte paper texture throughout. Keep the composition clean, balanced, elegant, and contemporary, with a premium editorial mood. Avoid excessive decoration, neon effects, or anything cartoonish.
```

### 5. Forest green + cream + grid/square mosaic

Intelligent, serious, slightly institutional.

**Prompt:**

```txt
Edit the provided image in a minimalist editorial collage style. Preserve the subject’s recognizable identity, facial features, and pose. Apply a high-contrast black-and-white portrait treatment with a subtle forest-green duotone tint. Use a solid black rectangular background behind the portrait for strong contrast and focus. Surround the portrait with an abstract composition of small square blocks, grid-like panels, and modular paper tiles in cream, bone, pale sage, muted olive, and deep forest green. The shapes should feel clean, structured, and print-inspired, with light layering and variation. Add a subtle paper grain texture so the image feels tactile and editorial. Keep the design refined, serious, and modern, with clear visual hierarchy and minimal clutter, like a high-end newspaper magazine cover.
```
